Video · Motion

United Airlines

Scope

CTV & YouTube Ad

Tools

After Effects · Premiere Pro

Format

30-second ad

Result

$100M+ client milestone

I proposed video advertising as a new creative channel for the agency — and when United Airlines agreed to pilot it, I owned the project end to end.

Working from United's motion toolkit and brand guidelines, I studied their existing YouTube content and social posts to understand how they already used motion. I produced a 30-second ad in Premiere Pro and After Effects, purpose-built for both YouTube and connected TV delivery. The logo animation was synced to the beat of the selected track. A slow zoom on the static hero image created the illusion of motion within a brand-approved asset. Copy pacing was carefully calibrated to ensure on-screen read time worked for large-format TV viewing. The ad was featured multiple times in company-wide marketing meetings.

The ad

United Airlines — CTV & YouTube, 30s, November 2024

Format considerations

YouTube

Viewed on screens ranging from mobile to desktop. Strong brand presence needed in the first 5 seconds before skip option. Copy timing calibrated for mixed viewing contexts.

Aspect ratio: 16:9 · Resolution: 1920×1080

CTV — Connected TV

Viewed on large living room screens from a distance. Scale and legibility drove layout decisions — type sizing, safe zones, and visual hierarchy were all adjusted for the 10-foot viewing experience.

Aspect ratio: 16:9 · Viewed at 6–10 ft distance

Production approach

01

Research & brand immersion

Studied United's brand guidelines, audited their YouTube channel and social posts to understand how they used motion before proposing a direction.

02

Motion toolkit integration

United provided a motion toolkit with pre-approved animated assets from their social posts. Adapted and extended these for a longer video format.

03

Logo animation & music sync

Animated the United logo beat-matched to the selected track — a small production detail that adds polish and energy to the open.

04

Static image motion technique

The hero image was brand-approved and static. Used a slow zoom (Ken Burns effect) to imply motion and maintain visual energy throughout the spot.
